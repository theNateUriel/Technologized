Introduction to ICT (Information and Communications Technology)

Information and Communication Technology is abbreviated as ICT. It includes digital hardware and tools like smartphones, laptops, tablets, and smartboards, as well as audio-visual equipment, projectors, and other technologies used in business, education, information, travel, and development. The internet, blended learning, online learning, social media, cloud computing, flipped classrooms, learning management systems, email, and opportunities for online learning are also included. In this contemporary time, a ton of mediations are being made for viable learning of the understudies and different ideas are utilized to depict these intercessions for the digitization of training in types of speculation, reception, and utilization of more up to date Data and Correspondence Innovation in instructive practices and exploration. Computer Assisted Instructions (CAI), Computer Assisted Learning (CAL), Computer Assisted Language Learning (CALL), Computer Assisted Teaching (CAT), Information and Communication Technology (IT), Information and Communication Technology (ICT), Instructional Technology, Teaching Learning Technology, and Educational Technology, among other terms, are examples of these concepts. A considerable lot of these ideas are utilized conversely overall as a result of the nearby comprehension they have.


ICT is a broad term that refers to any communication device or application, including satellite systems, radio-television, cellular, phone, computer, and network hardware and software, and other related services and applications. Berce, Lanfranco, and Vehovar (2008) define information and communication technology (ICT) as "a mixture of hardware (equipment) and software (operating system, applications, etc.)" and means of communication (Local Area Networks, Wide-Area Networks, Backbone Networks, etc.) According to Bates (2015), the term "information and communication technology" refers to all of the computer-based, software-based, or e-learning-supporting tools and equipment. As a result, the term "information and communication technology" is being used in this study to refer to both the newest technologies that are used in the digitalization of educational institutions and the everyday practices that teachers use to implement these technologies. In addition, information and communication technology (ICT) may refer to a variety of information (assignments, projects, reports), communication (e-mails, websites, online storage), administration (LMS, CMS, databases), and software (courseware, tutorials, databases, websites), as well as to devices (cell phones, tablets, computers, laptops, e-boards, projectors, and projectors with or without internet access). ICT is also defined as a tool by Wang and Woo (2007). "ICT can be hardware (like computers, digital cameras), software (like Excel, discussion forums, or both," they said. It mostly means various computer-based resources and tools (software) in the educational context.
